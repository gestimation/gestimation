Brew a coffee, start a conversation. As the steam rises and fades, time passes — and the world of statistics slowly opens up.

This site grew out of small coffee chats between a statistician father and his clinician daughter. Over coffee, they move back and forth between clinic and code, papers and everyday life — turning “I sort of get this” moments into questions they can actually work through.

Even when the topic is a difficult statistical method or a messy formula, we try to explain it in words with warmth, like the heat from a mug on a winter evening.
Each story can be reproduced with R code. Whether you want to learn deeply or just pause for a moment, you can read these as short essays for quiet nights — at your own pace.

How to read this site

  • If you start with Study design I–V,
    you will get a first outline of “what question to ask, and what data to collect to answer it.”
  • If you want to choose by interest:
    • To build a better footing for reading papers → Frequentist thinking / Effects and time
    • To tidy up your analysis practice → Adjusting for bias
    • To organize how you think about causality → Causal inference
    • To eventually publish your work as a paper → The Craft of Research
  • Each episode is written so that you can read it on its own.
    But if you read across the series, a mental map of “what research is as a practice” starts to form in the background.

Pick any place that catches your eye, grab your favourite drink, and read at the pace that feels right for you.
